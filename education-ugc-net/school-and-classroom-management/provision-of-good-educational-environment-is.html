SCHOOL AND CLASSROOM MANAGEMENT

[SOURCES]
Provision of good educational environment is

(A) ** Non instructional tasks

(B) Instrucitonal tasks

(C) Both

(D) None

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Noninstructional duties include those duties that are not directly related to the instruction. Examples include but are not limited to bus duty, car duty, cafeteria duty and/or building and hallway duty, etc. to provide for the safety and proper supervision of students.

Concept note-2: -Non-instructional records are accounts of events or activities that are not related to teaching or learning, such as taking attendance, collecting lunch count, and organizing field trip permission slips and money.

Concept note-3: -For the purpose of this section, “non-instructional duty” means the supervision of students before or after the instructional day begins or ends for students or for the supervision of students during breakfasts, lunches, recesses, or scheduled breaks.

Concept note-4: -There are three types of learning environments. These types, also known as categories or areas, are the physical setting, psychological setting, and instructional setting.
