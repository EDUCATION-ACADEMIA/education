The summative evaluation is?

(A) Continous

(B) Diagnostic

(C) ** Cerifying judgment

(D) None

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -"Summative Assessment", commonly known as “Assessment of Learning” is a type of assessment which: measures, certifies, and reports the level of students’ learning by assessing them at the end of the term.

Concept note-2: -Hence, we conclude that Summative evaluation is a term-end/closure end evaluation which is judgemental in nature.

Concept note-3: -Summative assessment is the culminating evaluation of student work at the end of a term or course. Unlike formative assessment methods, students do not receive feedback regarding their progress with summative evaluation. Examples of summative assessment include: Exam. Peer-Evaluation.

Concept note-4: -The goal of summative assessment is to evaluate student learning at the end of an instructional unit by comparing it against some standard or benchmark. Summative assessments are often high stakes, which means that they have a high point value. Examples of summative assessments include: a midterm exam.

Concept note-5: -Here the statement that ‘summative assessment is called formal assessment’ is correct about the continuous and comprehensive evaluation of learner.
