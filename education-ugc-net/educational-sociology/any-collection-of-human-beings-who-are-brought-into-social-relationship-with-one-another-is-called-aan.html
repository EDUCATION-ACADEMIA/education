Any collection of human beings who are brought into social relationship with one another, is called a/an

(A) Office

(B) ** Group

(C) Family

(D) Institution

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the social sciences, a social group is defined as two or more people who interact with one another, share similar characteristics, and collectively have a sense of unity.

Concept note-2: -Group means a collection of human beings who have social relationships with one another. Social relationship involves some degree of reciprocity as well as awareness of mutuality.

Concept note-3: -A population refers to any collection of specified group of human beings or of non-human entities such as objects, educational institutions, time units, geographical areas, prices of wheat or salaries drawn by individuals. Some statisticians call it universe.

Concept note-4: -Definition. Broadly defined, social relationships refer to the connections that exist between people who have recurring interactions that are perceived by the participants to have personal meaning.

Concept note-5: -Examples of social relationships include coworkers, distant relatives, and acquaintances. Another distinction useful for categorizing relationships is whether or not they are voluntary.
