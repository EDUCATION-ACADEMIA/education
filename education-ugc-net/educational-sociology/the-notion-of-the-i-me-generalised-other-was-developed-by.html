The notion of the “I: me: Generalised other” was developed by

(A) Mead GH

(B) ** Morton Robert

(C) Thomas William

(D) Cooley Charles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The generalized other is one of George Herbert Mead’s central concepts in his seminal discussion of the social genesis of the self. According to Mead, the self resides in the individual’s ability to take account of himself or herself as a social being.

Concept note-2: -According to Mead, the generalized other is the vehicle by which we are linked to society.

Concept note-3: -The definition of ‘’generalized other” is ‘’the completion of childhood development, in which children can perceive the perspectives of others. ‘’ This is the stage in which children begin to perceive societal expectations and change their actions according to those expectations.
