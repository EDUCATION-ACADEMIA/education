MOTIVATION AND EMOTION

[SOURCES]
The”need for success”expectancy for success and the”incentive value of success”and three motivational factors which determine the strength of.

(A) Social Motives

(B) Biological Motives

(C) Personal Motives

(D) ** Achievement Need

(E) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In other words, one’s tendency to approach success (Ts) is a product of three motivational factors: the need for achievement, also known as the motive for success, the probability of success and the incentive value of success.

Concept note-2: -Love and belonging needs, esteem needs, and self-actualization needs are the three features of achievement motivation.

Concept note-3: -Atkinson’s developed formula for achievement motivation entails three factors; namely, motive of individual, probability of success of a given task and the incentives or reward given for that task.

Concept note-4: -Incentive value of success – the intrinsic value experienced by the individual after success has been achieved; the harder the task, the greater the incentive value as probability of success is reduced.

Concept note-5: -Expectancy-Value Theory is a theory of motivation that describes the relationship between a student’s expectancy for success at a task or the achievement of a goal in relation to the value of task completion or goal attainment. Expectancy refers to a student’s expectation for success on a given task.
