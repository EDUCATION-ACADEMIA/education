Corpus luteum and placenta of pregnant women secret the hormone.

(A) Estrogens

(B) Androgens

(C) ** Progestins

(D) Adrenalin

(E) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Progesterone is an endogenous steroid hormone that is commonly produced by the adrenal cortex as well as the gonads, which consist of the ovaries and the testes. Progesterone is also secreted by the ovarian corpus luteum during the first ten weeks of pregnancy, followed by the placenta in the later phase of pregnancy.

Concept note-2: -Progesterone acts to maintain pregnancy by supporting the lining of the uterus (womb), which provides the environment for the foetus and the placenta to grow. Progesterone prevents the shedding of this lining (similar to that which occurs at the end of a menstrual cycle), since this would result in pregnancy loss.

Concept note-3: -Your corpus luteum produces the hormone progesterone that makes your uterus a healthy environment for a developing fetus. A new corpus luteum forms each time you ovulate and breaks down once you no longer need it to make progesterone.

Concept note-4: -Progesterone. This hormone is produced by the ovaries and by the placenta during pregnancy. Progesterone stimulates the thickening of the uterine lining in anticipation of implantation of a fertilized egg.
