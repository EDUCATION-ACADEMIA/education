Verbal guidance is least effective in the learning of:

(A) ** Skills

(B) Attitudes

(C) Aptitudes

(D) Relationship

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, it could be concluded that Oral guidance is less effective in Teaching skills.

Concept note-2: -Hence, it could be concluded that verbal guidance is least effective in the learning of Skills.

Concept note-3: -Verbal guidance is least effective in teaching skills.

Concept note-4: -Disadvantages. Learners can only remember a limited amount of spoken information. Needs to be simple and short for novices and younger performers. Difficult to communicate subtleties and complexities of tactics.

Concept note-5: -Quick Reference. Coaching technique in which athletes are instructed to do exactly what they hear the coach (or cassette, etc.) say they should do. Verbal guidance often involves the use of simple, clear, and meaningful cues.
