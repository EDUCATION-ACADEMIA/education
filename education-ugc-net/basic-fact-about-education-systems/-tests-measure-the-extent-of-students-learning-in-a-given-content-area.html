____ tests measure the extent of students learning in a given content area.

(A) Aptitude

(B) Diagnostic

(C) Readiness

(D) ** Achievement

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Achievement tests are universally used in the classroom to measure whether students possess the pre-requisite skills needed to succeed in any unit or whether the students have achieved the objective of the planned instruction. They monitor the students learning and identify learning difficulties among students.

Concept note-2: -An achievement test is designed to measure a person’s level of skill, accomplishment, or knowledge in a specific area.

Concept note-3: -The term achievement tests refers to tests designed to measure the knowledge, skills, and abilities attained by a test taker in a field, in a subject area, or in a content domain in which the test taker has received training or instruction.

Concept note-4: -Criterion referenced test–a test which measures a student’s performance according to a particular standard or criterion which has been agreed upon.

Concept note-5: -Summative assessments-tests, quizzes, and other graded course activities that are used to measure student performance. They are cumulative and often reveal what students have learned at the end of a unit or the end of a course.
