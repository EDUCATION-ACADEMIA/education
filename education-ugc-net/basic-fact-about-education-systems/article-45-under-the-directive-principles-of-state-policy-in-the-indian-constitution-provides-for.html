Article 45 under the Directive Principles of State policy in the Indian Constitution, provides for

(A) Free and compulsory primary education

(B) ** Education for weaker sections of the country

(C) Giving financial assistance to less advanced states

(D) Rights of minorities to establish educational institutions

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -*[45. The State shall endeavour to provide, within a period of ten years from the commencement of this Constitution, for free and compulsory education for all children until they complete the age of fourteen years.]

Concept note-2: -The Constitution of India in a Directive Principle contained in article 45, has â€˜made a provision for free and compulsory education for all children up to the age of fourteen years within ten years of promulgation of the Constitution.

Concept note-3: -Article 45 in The Constitution Of India 1949.

Concept note-4: -The Constitution (Eighty-sixth Amendment) Act, 2002 inserted Article 21-A in the Constitution of India to provide free and compulsory education of all children in the age group of six to fourteen years as a Fundamental Right in such a manner as the State may, by law, determine.

Concept note-5: -It also ensures equality in educational opportunities in India. Insertion of Article 21A, making elementary education a fundamental right of all children between the age group of 6-14 years, Article 45 has been amended so as to restrict its scope to pre-primary education up to 6 years of age.
