In which way, the Secondary Education will change into?

(A) Higher Education

(B) Degree level

(C) ** According to time and conditions

(D) Both A and B

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -New Changes In the Higher Education System In India NEP 2020 aims at increasing the Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER) from 26.3% to 50% by 2035. The policy has largely focused on introducing flexible, holistic, as well as multidisciplinary undergraduate courses with multiple exit options.

Concept note-2: -Considering the pace of technological advancement, it won’t be surprising if, by 2050, at least 90 per cent of the student population is pursuing online educational programs. AdvertisementWhy this Ad? This will include online schooling as well.

Concept note-3: -While technical skills will remain essential, soft skills will gain prominence, making them an integral part of learning. Skills such as complex problem solving, creativity, innovation, analytical thinking, emotional intelligence, and empathy will be critical in a future-proof curriculum.

Concept note-4: -Education was strong in ancient days also. In ancient day’s education system changed based on the periods such as Vedic, Brahmanical, Muslim, British periods. In Vedic periods schools were becoming boarding and students were handed over to teacher. Physical education was compulsory at that period.
