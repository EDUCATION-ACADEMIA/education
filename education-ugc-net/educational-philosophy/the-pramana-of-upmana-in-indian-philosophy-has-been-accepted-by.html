The Pramana of Upmana in Indian philosophy has been accepted by

(A) Nyaya

(B) Vedanta

(C) Mimamsa

(D) ** All of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nyaya, Vedanta and Mimamsa schools all accept upamana as a pramana.

Concept note-2: -The accepted number of pramana varies, according to the philosophical system or school; the exegetic system of Mimamsa accepts five, whereas Vedanta as a whole proposes three.

Concept note-3: -upamana, (Sanskrit: “comparison”) in Indian philosophy, the fourth of the five means (pramanas) by which one can have valid cognitions of the world. Upamana describes knowledge imparted by means of analogy.

Concept note-4: -These are perception, inference, comparison, verbal testimony, postulation and non-perception. Of them the Carvaka school claims that perception is the only pramana or dependable source of knowledge.
