EDUCATIONAL PHILOSOPHY

[SOURCES]
The most important trait of philosophy is

(A) Analysis

(B) ** Criticism

(C) Synthesis

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The most essential characteristic of a philosopher, or any academic really, is innate (often insatiable) curiosity.

Concept note-2: -Criticism in philosophy is a matter of employing a critical methodology which, in seeking to detect problems and difficulties in working out a philosophi-cal position, looks to ways of overcoming or bypassing them.

Concept note-3: -Criticism, according to kant, is an investigation of the nature and the limits of reason and knowledge pursued in such a manner that both skepti cism and dogmatism are avoided.

Concept note-4: -Four characteristics of philosophical thinking are wonder, contemplation, reason, and intellectual independence. Wonder is when we stop taking things for granted, marvel at them, and ask questions about them. Philosophical thinking involves contemplation, which is giving something your attention.
