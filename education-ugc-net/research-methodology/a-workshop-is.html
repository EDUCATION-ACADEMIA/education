[SOURCES]
A workshop is

(A) a meeting for discussion on a topic

(B) a conference for discussion on a topic

(C) a class at a college or a university in which a teacher and the students discuss a topic

(D) ** a brief intensive course for a small group emphasizing the development of a skill or technique for solving a specific problem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, we can conclude that the workshop is a brief intensive course for a small group emphasizing the development of a skill or technique for solving a specific problem. Seminar-It is a form of academic instruction, either at an academic institution or professional organization.

Concept note-2: -Workshops are interactive sessions or small events, usually, a day or two long, dedicated to discussing on practical work, on a specific topic in which a group of people engage in brainstorming, problem-solving and knowledge-sharing.

Concept note-3: -A workshop may introduce a new idea, inspire participants to further explore it on their own, or may illustrate and promote actual process practice. It is a great way to teach hands-on skills as it gives learners an opportunity to try out new methods and fail in a safe environment.

Concept note-4: -A workshop is an interactive meeting in which a group of people goes through a series of activities to achieve to solve a problem or work on a project. Workshops are often led by a facilitator and can range from a couple of hours to multiple days. This is our go-to definition of a workshop.

Concept note-5: -Difference between seminar and workshop Seminars are usually organized around a specific topic, while workshops can be more general or tailored to a particular audience. Seminars are typically shorter than workshops, lasting anywhere from a few hours to a few days, while workshops can last for days or even weeks.
