Which one of the following policy document was called the ‘Magna Carta’ of Western education system in India?

(A) Ma Caulay’s Minute of 1835

(B) Indian Education Commission Reportof 1882

(C) ** Charles Woods Despatch of 1854

(D) Releigh Commission Reportof 1902

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Wood’s Dispatch on Education, 1854. The Wood’s Despatch is considered as ‘Magna-Carta’ of English Education in India. Charles Wood was the President of the Board of Control of East India Company.

Concept note-2: -The Wood’s Despatch is known as the ‘Magna Carta’ of English education in India.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: Wood’s dispatch was called the Magna Carta of Indian Education because it aimed to give more education liberties and freedoms to the people of India and advance their education system.

Concept note-4: -In 1854 he sent a despatch to Lord Dalhousie, the then Governor-General of India. Wood’s despatch suggested that primary schools must Adopt vernacular languages. Through the despatch, he also suggested that high schools use anglo-vernacular medium and that English should be the medium for college-level education.

Concept note-5: -In 1854, Sir Charles Wood, the President of the Board of Control of the British East India Company, sent a formal dispatch to Lord Dalhousie, the then Governor-General of India, suggesting a large shift to English language use within India.
