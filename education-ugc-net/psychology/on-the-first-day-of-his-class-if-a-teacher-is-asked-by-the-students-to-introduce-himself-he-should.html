On the first day of his class, if a teacher is asked by the students to introduce himself, he should

(A) ask them to meet after the class

(B) ** tell them about himself in brief

(C) ignore the demand and start teaching

(D) scold the student for this unwanted demand

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Thanks for giving me this opportunity to introduce myself. My name is [your first name]. I’m from [name of the city] where I finished my schooling last year from [name of the school]. Is there anyone here from my city? (Changes tack to engage with the audience.)

Concept note-2: -Introducing Yourself as a Teacher Include your name (what you would like them to address you by), your academic background, and your interests. You could say something like, “Good morning class, my name is John Smith, you can call me John or Professor Smith.

Concept note-3: -Make sure to start off your letter by introducing yourself, perhaps what school/class you come from, and a little bit about yourself. This could include likes, hobbies, sports, arts, etc. Be open, especially making sure to include things you, and others, find unique in you.

Concept note-4: -Appearance. Name Details. Personal Interests and Hobbies. Explain Classroom Rules. Be Courteous and Positive. Invite Questions and Responses. 25-Jun-2018
