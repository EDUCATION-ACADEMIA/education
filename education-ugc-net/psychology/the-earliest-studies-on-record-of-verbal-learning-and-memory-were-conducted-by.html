The earliest studies on record of verbal learning and memory were conducted by:

(A) MOWRER

(B) PAVLOV

(C) SKINNER

(D) ** EBBINGHAUS

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hermann Ebbinghaus: The First Psychologist to Study Learning and Memory.

Concept note-2: -Hermann Ebbinghaus (24 January 1850 – 26 February 1909) was a German psychologist who pioneered the experimental study of memory, and is known for his discovery of the forgetting curve and the spacing effect.

Concept note-3: -Verbal learning is the process of actively memorizing new material using mental pictures, associations, and other activities. Verbal learning was first studied by Hermann Ebbinghaus, who used lists of nonsense syllables to test recall.

Concept note-4: -Ebbinghaus experimented with his own ability to remember using a list of nonsense syllables, which he attempted to recall after different lengths of time. His experiences and results revealed a number of key aspects of memory: Memories weaken over time.
