In CCE, Formative and Summative Assessment totals to-

(A) ** 40% and 60% respectively

(B) 60% and 40% respectively

(C) 50% and 50% respectively

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The periodicity of the two types of assessment are four and twice a year respectively. Formative Assessment totals to 40% weightage. Summative Assessment totals to 60% weightage. There are nine grades in Part A of Scholastic assessment and Part B of the same assessment has five grades.

Concept note-2: -Students will be evaluated in scholastic areas both by Formative and Summative assessments with the weightage of 40 and 60 percent marks respectively.

Concept note-3: -Hence, we conclude that in CCE, 50% is of formative assessment.

Concept note-4: -For example, formative assessments may have a weightage of 20%, while summative assessments may have a weightage of 80%. The grade chart also includes the range of marks for each grade, such as A, B, C, and so on. This helps teachers to assign grades to students based on their performance in the evaluations.

Concept note-5: -Formative assessments have low stakes and usually carry no grade, which in some instances may discourage the students from doing the task or fully engaging with it. The goal of summative assessment is to evaluate student learning at the end of an instructional unit by comparing it against some standard or benchmark.
