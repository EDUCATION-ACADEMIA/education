A person can enjoy teaching as a profession when he

(A) has control over students.

(B) ** commands respect from students.

(C) is more qualified than his colleagues.

(D) is very close to higher authorities.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The teacher is committed to empowering and shaping young minds. The profession offers a dignified position in society. The profession commands respect and trust from the stakeholders. There is a scope of autonomy in the profession.

Concept note-2: -Earning your students’ respect requires you to be consistent, keep your word, control your frustration, learn who your students are, be patient, speak in a normal tone of voice, and be kind.

Concept note-3: -Respect in the classroom boosts teacher effectiveness and encourages active and appropriate participation in classroom activities. Positive, supportive, and respectful relationships between teachers and students even increase students’ odds of long-term academic and social success.

Concept note-4: -Listen to students-and hear them. Use positive humor, not sarcasm. Provide corrective feedback in ways that foster student effort. Acknowledge student growth.

Concept note-5: -Teachers must model strong character traits, including perseverance, honesty, respect, lawfulness, fairness, patience, and unity. As an educator, teachers must treat every student with kindness and respect without showing any favoritism, prejudice or partiality.
