The theory that we all experience a series of developmental crisis throughout our lives was proposed by:

(A) FRUED

(B) ADLER

(C) SHELDON

(D) ** ERICKSON

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Erik Erikson’s (1958, 1963) psychosocial development theory proposes that our personality develops through eight stages, from infancy to old age.

Concept note-2: -Erik Erikson first published his eight-stage theory of human development in his 1950 book Childhood and Society.

Concept note-3: -According to Erik Erikson, a crisis is an existential challenge that people experience several times throughout their lives, and such experiences will influence people’s personality and identity development.

Concept note-4: -Erikson’s theory suggests that your ego identity develops throughout your entire life during eight specific stages: Infancy – Basic trust versus mistrust. Toddler – Autonomy versus shame and doubt. Preschool-age – Initiative versus guilt.
