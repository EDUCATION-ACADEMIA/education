When did Sir Syed Ahmad Khan resign the government service?

(A) in 1877

(B) in 1879

(C) ** in 1876

(D) in 1875

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sir Syed retired from government service in 1876 and settled in Aligarh. In 1878, he was nominated as an additional member of the Imperial Legislative Council, which he served from July 1878 to July 1880.

Concept note-2: -This led Sir Syed to initiate a movement for the intellectual, educational, social and cultural regeneration of the Muslim society. This movement came to be known as the Aligarh movement after Sir Syed established his school at Aligarh which later became the center of the movement.

Concept note-3: -One example was when the Congress demanded political representation according to the population. This meant that the Hindu majority would be in power and Sir Syed felt that the wider interests of the Muslims, especially in terms of education, would be neglected. Thus he opposed the Indian National Congress.

Concept note-4: -Sir Syed Ahmed Khan is associated with the Aligarh Movement. He organised and founded the Aligarh Movement in 1875. It was based in Mohammedan Anglo-Oriental College, Aligarh.
