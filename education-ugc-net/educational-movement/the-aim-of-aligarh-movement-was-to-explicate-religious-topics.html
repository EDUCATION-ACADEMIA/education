The aim of Aligarh Movement was to explicate religious topics____

(A) Elaborately

(B) Truly

(C) ** Scientifically

(D) Harmoniously

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The main objective of the Aligarh movement was to persuade the Muslim community to acquire modern knowledge and the English laguage. In addition it had the following additional objectives: To build trust between the government and the Muslim community.

Concept note-2: -He instituted Scientific Society in 1863 to instill a scientific temperament into the Muslims and to make the Western knowledge available to Indians in their own language.

Concept note-3: -History of the Aligarh Movement The condition of the Muslim society was found to be culturally, socially, and educationally backward by Sir Syed Ahmed Khan. This movement was initiated to regenerate Muslim society and initiate intellectual upliftment in Muslim society.

Concept note-4: -The Aligarh movement aimed at establishing a modern system of education for the Muslim community in British India. The movement derived its name from the North Indian city of Aligarh, from where it originated. The movement started with the foundation of the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College in 1875.

Concept note-5: -The Aligarh movement was a social reform movement in India that was launched by Sir Syed Ahmed Khan in 1875. The primary goals of the movement were to promote Western education among the Muslim community and to work for the upliftment of Muslims in the country.
