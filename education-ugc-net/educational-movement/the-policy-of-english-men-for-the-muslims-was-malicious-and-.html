The policy of English men for the Muslims was malicious and ____

(A) Forgiving

(B) Lenient

(C) ** Vindictive

(D) Gracious

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The British government consciously discriminated against the Muslims, holding them responsible for the uprising of 1857. They also took part in Wahabi Movement. These events made the British look at Muslims with suspicion which resulted in indiscriminate treatment towards the Muslims.

Concept note-2: -They were required to shift to a place near the Qutab Minar. In 1856, Lord Canning announced that after the death of Bahadur Shah, his successors would not be allowed to use imperial titles with their names. These decisions of the British hurt the feelings of the Muslims.

Concept note-3: -Complete answer: The British treat Muslims badly after the 1857 rebellion because they accuse them of being involved in the Revolt of 1857, which was organised as an all-India organisation to form a broad-based liberation campaign.
