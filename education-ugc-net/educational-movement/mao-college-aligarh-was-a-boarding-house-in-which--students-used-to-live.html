M.A.O College Aligarh was a boarding house in which ____ students used to live.

(A) 30

(B) ** 25

(C) 40

(D) 95

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The statesman Syed Ahmad Khan founded the predecessor of Aligarh Muslim University, the Muhammadan Anglo Oriental College, in 1875 having already established two schools. These were part of the movement of Muslim awakening associated with Syed Ahmad Khan which came to be known as Aligarh Movement.

Concept note-2: -Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College was founded in 1875. The college started as a primary school on Queen Victoriaâ€™s birthday, 24 May 1875 known as Muhammedan Anglo-Oriental Collegiate School. It was established as Madrasatul Uloom Musalmanan-e-Hind in 1875 and after two years it became Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College.

Concept note-3: -On 24 May 1875, Sir Syed founded the Madarsatul Uloom in Aligarh and patterned the MAO College after Oxford and Cambridge universities that he went on a trip to London.

Concept note-4: -In 1933, MAO College was established by the Anjuman-e-Islamia in Amritsar on the pattern of the MAO College Aligarh to overcome the deficiencies of modern education in local Muslims. Professor S. Mercado was appointed the first principal.
