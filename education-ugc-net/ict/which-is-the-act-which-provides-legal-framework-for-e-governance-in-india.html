Which is the Act which provides legal framework for e-Governance in India

(A) ** IT Act 2000

(B) Indian Penal Code

(C) IT (amendment) Act 2008

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -to promote efficient delivery of Government services by means of reliable electronic records. (1) This Act may be called the Information Technology Act, 2000.

Concept note-2: -The act that provides the legal framework for e-Governance in India is the IT Act 2000.

Concept note-3: -According to the World Bank, E-Governance is when government agencies use information and communication technologies to transform relations with citizens, businesses, and other government agencies. One of the prime objectives of the IT Act, 2000 is the promotion of electronic governance.

Concept note-4: -21 OF 2000 [9th June, 2000.] An Act to provide legal recognition for transactions carried out by means of electronic data. interchange and other means of electronic communication, commonly referred to as. ―electronic commerce‖, which involve the use of alternatives to paper-based methods of.

Concept note-5: -The Information Technology Act, 2000 (also known as ITA-2000, or the IT Act) is an Act of the Indian Parliament (No 21 of 2000) notified on 17 October 2000. It is the primary law in India dealing with cybercrime and electronic commerce.
