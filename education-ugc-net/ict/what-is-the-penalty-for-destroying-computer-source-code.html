What is the penalty for destroying computer source code

(A) Two year imprisonment or 2 lakh rupees penalty or both

(B) ** Three year imprisonment or 2 lakh rupees penalty or both

(C) Three year imprisonment or 3 lakh rupees penalty or both

(D) Three year imprisonment or 5 lakh rupees penalty or both

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Held: The court held that Tampering with Source code are punishable with three years jail and or two lakh rupees fine of rupees two lakh rupees for altering, concealing and destroying the source code.

Concept note-2: -Some important sections of the IT Act under which cyber crimes may be registered are : Section 65 – Tampering with Computer Source Documents. Penalties if found guilty can be imprisonment up to 3 years and/or up-to Rs 2 lakh fine.

Concept note-3: -he shall be liable to pay damages by way of compensation not exceeding one crore rupees to the person so affected.

Concept note-4: -Offences under Information Technology Act, 2000. Section 65: Any person tamper, conceal, destroy, or alter any computer source document intentionally, then he shall be liable to pay penalty upto Rs. 2, 00, 000/-, or Imprisonment upto 3 years, or both.

Concept note-5: -The penalty for such acts is imprisonment and fines up to 2 years and Rs. 2000, respectively. The punishment for any of the above crimes may be up to five years of imprisonment and a fine of up to Rs. 5000 for repeat (second-time) offenders.
