CHILD DEVELOPMENT AND PEDAGOGY

[SOURCES]
If student is too shy to participate in the class you will.

(A) ** Not ask questions to him

(B) Ask only those questions from him whose answers can be given by him

(C) Not ask those questions from him whose answers are beyond his means and due to which he may become an object of ridicule in the class

(D) Ask questions to him only when he is keen to answer them

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Try small group discussions. Divide students into partners or small groups and have them discuss the topic. It may be less intimidating for some students than answering questions in front of the entire class. Visit each group to check in with them and see how the discussion is going.

Concept note-2: -Remember They’re Not Doing This on Purpose. Start with Non-Verbal Communication. Let Them Move at Their Own Pace. Use Positive Reinforcement. Learn Their Triggers. Avoid Labeling Them. Create Safe Spaces With Friends. Give Them a Job. More items •08-Jun-2022

Concept note-3: -say that the question is wrong. say that the Information will be given upon receipt. try to get the answer out of the class itself. give it to the student for homework. 30-Dec-2022

Concept note-4: -Don’t put pressure on them to come up with a response quickly. Change the topic to suit the student’s ability. Give a list of words with meanings to learn and use. Conduct special classes to practice speaking.
