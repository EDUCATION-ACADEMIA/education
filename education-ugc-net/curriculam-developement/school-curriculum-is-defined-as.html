CURRICULAM DEVELOPEMENT

[SOURCES]
School curriculum is defined as

(A) Methods of instruction

(B) ** Experiences organized by school

(C) Materials of instruction

(D) Courses of study

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A school curriculum is a complete set of organized experiences in the school. It includes aims, objectives, teaching content, teaching strategies, and all teaching-learning aids to cater totality of students’ experiences that occur in the educational process.

Concept note-2: -Hollis Caswell (1901-1989) He believes that subject matter is developed around the interest of the learners and their social functions. So, the curriculum is a set of experiences. Learners must experience what they learn.

Concept note-3: -Morroe – “Curriculum includes all those activities which are utilised by the school to attain the aims of education”.

Concept note-4: -Hass (1980): The curriculum is all of the experiences that individual learners have in a program of education whose purpose is to achieve broad goals and related specific objectives, which is planned in terms of a framework of theory and research or past and present professional practice.
