CURRICULAM DEVELOPEMENT

[SOURCES]
Syllabus is a part of:

(A) ** Curriculum

(B) Classroom

(C) Activities

(D) Society

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A curriculum is a set of courses and contents offered in an educational institution. A syllabus is a descriptive list of subjects to be taught in a class. The curriculum contains course content, objectives, methodologies, etc. The syllabus contains a list of topics to cover, details about assignments, assessments, etc.

Concept note-2: -So, option 4 (Goals, curriculum outline, objectives, content, instructional strategies, and evaluation strategies) is the correct one. In summary, the components of a curriculum are distinct but interrelated to each other. These four components should be always present in a curriculum.

Concept note-3: -Syllabus is the document that contains all the portion of the concepts covered in a subject. Curriculum is the overall content, taught in an educational system or a course.

Concept note-4: -Learning experiences Thus from the above-mentioned points, it is clear that teaching is not a component of the curriculum.

Concept note-5: -Any curriculum consists of several components: objectives, attitudes, time, students and teachers, needs analysis, classroom activities, materials, study skills, language skills, vocabulary, grammar and assessment.
