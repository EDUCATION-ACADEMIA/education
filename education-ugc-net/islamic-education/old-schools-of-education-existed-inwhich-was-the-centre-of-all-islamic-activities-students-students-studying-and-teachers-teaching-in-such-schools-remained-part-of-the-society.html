ISLAMIC EDUCATION

[SOURCES]
Old schools of education existed in____which was the centre of all Islamic activities students students studying and teachers teaching in such schools remained part of the society.

(A) Madrisas

(B) Vales

(C) Grounds

(D) ** Mosques

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Islamic Empire, spanning for almost 1, 000 years, saw at least 60 major learning centers throughout the Middle East and North Africa, some of the most prominent among these being Baghdad in the East and Cordoba in the West.

Concept note-2: -madrasah, (Arabic: “school”) English madrassa, Turkish medrese, institution of higher education in the Islamic sciences (ʿulūm; singular, ʿilm).

Concept note-3: -The Abbasid caliphs established the city of Baghdad in 762 CE. It became a center of learning and the hub of what is known as the Golden Age of Islam.

Concept note-4: -. Education during Muslim period was conducted at religious places, which were commonly attached to Masjids. Education was provided free and precise. Medieval education was the foundation for the development of Islamic social morals which were based on Islamic doctrines, social traditions and political principles.
