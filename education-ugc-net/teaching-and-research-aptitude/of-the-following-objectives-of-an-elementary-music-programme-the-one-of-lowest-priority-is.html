Of the following objectives of an Elementary Music Programme the one of lowest priority is

(A) To provide children with emotion outlets.

(B) To prepare children for wise use of leisure time.

(C) ** To develop a group of performers for Hiliday Assemblies.

(D) To expose children to a variety of musical compositions.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fundamental in a student’s overall social, emotional, and cognitive development, music enhances reasoning, inspires creativity, and encourages critical thinking, self-discipline, problem-solving, decision-making, cooperation, and imagination.

Concept note-2: -awareness and recognition of the local art forms. know the main Music, Dance forms of India. promote nationalism and national integration through celebration of important days, national, social and religious festivals and occasions. promote interest in and motivation for music and music related activities.

Concept note-3: -Especially in young children, music directly benefits the ability to learn words, speak them correctly, and process the many new sounds they hear from others. Music education involves a high level of memorization. Students must be able to read music by sight, play the proper notes on their instrument or recall lyrics.

Concept note-4: -Music objectives in the psychomotor domain make part of the majority of musical activities, especially singing and playing instruments, as well as expressing musical experience with movement and art. They have an important influence on the formation of children’s sound concepts.
