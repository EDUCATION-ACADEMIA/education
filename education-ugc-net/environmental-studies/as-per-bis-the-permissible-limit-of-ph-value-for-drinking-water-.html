As per BIS, the permissible limit of PH value for Drinking water :

(A) 6.5 to 7.5

(B) 7.0

(C) ** 6.5 to 8.5

(D) 6.0 to 8.5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Permissible limit: For drinking water, permissible limit of pH is 7 to 8.5, but it should not be less than 6.5 and not greater than 9.2, hence Permissible values of pH for public supplies may range between 6.6 to 8.5.

Concept note-2: -The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14. In general, a water with a pH < 7 is considered acidic and with a pH > 7 is considered basic. The normal range for pH in surface water systems is 6.5 to 8.5 and for groundwater systems 6 to 8.5.

Concept note-3: -There has been no evidence of harm found in drinking water with a pH of between 7 and 8.5. (Interesting to note: The pH of human blood is a little on the basic side, 7.365.) When the PH of water becomes greater than 8.5, water taste can become more bitter.

Concept note-4: -Water with a pH level between 6 and 8.5 is safe to drink because it is neither acidic nor alkaline enough to be dangerous in the human body. Water with a pH of less than 6 can be corrosive and filled with toxic metals.
