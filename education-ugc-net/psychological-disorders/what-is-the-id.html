PSYCHOLOGICAL DISORDERS

[SOURCES]
What is the Id?

(A) Part of the psyche that controls impulses

(B) Part of the psyche that reduces anxiety

(C) ** A description of innate instinctual needs

(D) Part of the psyche that controls our morals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Id: In psychoanalysis, the concept used to describe innate instinctual needs-especially sexual needs.

Concept note-2: -Axis I disorders tend to be the most commonly found in the public. They include anxiety disorders, such as panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and post-traumatic stress disorder. Other examples of Axis I disorders are as follows: Dissociative disorders. Eating disorders (anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, etc.)

Concept note-3: -Existential-humanistic psychology emphasizes the importance of human choices and decisions and feelings of awe toward life.

Concept note-4: -According to Sigmund Freud, the Superego. a. is the source of psychic motivation, such as sex and aggression.

Concept note-5: -The DSM is admittedly and proudly “atheoretical.” That means it remains neutral to all those pesky quibbling theories of why disorder exists, and instead classifies and defines disorder by symptoms and syndromes, i.e., what the disorders look like.
